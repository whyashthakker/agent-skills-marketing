# Review Generation Strategy

Design a repeatable process for earning more credible customer reviews. Focus on timing and prompts that yield authentic, useful reviews.

## Quick Reference

**Key Insight:** Ask when the customer just achieved a result or recognized value. Avoid asking too early or immediately after friction.

## When to Activate

Activate when the user asks to:
- generate more reviews
- improve review quality
- plan review acquisition
- design review prompts or timing

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define Channel and Goal

Identify:
- review channel (G2, Capterra, Google, Trustpilot, etc.)
- goal (volume, quality, specific use case)
- product or service type

### Step 2: Timing

Reference [references/review-timing.md](./references/review-timing.md):

**After Success Moment** – When the customer just achieved a result or recognized value

**After Support Resolution** – When trust was reinforced by support

**After Campaign Result** – When the product has measurable outcome milestones

**Avoid** – Too early, or immediately after friction unless clearly satisfied

### Step 3: Prompts

Design prompts that:
- are specific (not "How was your experience?")
- guide toward useful detail (outcome, comparison, use case)
- are short and easy to complete
- work across channels

### Step 4: Response Framework

Plan how to respond to reviews:
- positive: thank, ask for permission to use
- negative: acknowledge, resolve, follow up
- neutral: invite more detail

### Step 5: Output Structure

Produce:
- timing triggers and triggers to avoid
- prompt templates by channel
- response framework
- operational cadence

## Quality Gates

Before finalizing, verify:
- [ ] Timing aligns with success moments
- [ ] Prompts guide toward useful content
- [ ] Response framework is clear
- [ ] Low-value timing is avoided
